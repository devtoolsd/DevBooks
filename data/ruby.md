# Top Books on Ruby

- **[The Well-Grounded Rubyist, 3rd Ed.](https://www.manning.com/books/the-well-grounded-rubyist-third-edition)** - *David A. Black & Joseph Leo III* - *2019* - The best "understand Ruby deeply" book: objects, blocks, modules, and metaprogramming.
- **[Practical Object-Oriented Design, 2nd Ed.](https://www.poodr.com/)** - *Sandi Metz* - *2018* - Uses Ruby to teach OOP clearly; one of the best object-oriented design books for any language.
- **[Programming Ruby 3.2, 5th Ed.](https://pragprog.com/titles/ruby5/programming-ruby-3-2-5th-edition/)** - *David Thomas, Chad Fowler, Andy Hunt* - *2023* - The definitive Ruby reference; "the Pickaxe."
- **[Ruby on Rails Tutorial, 7th Ed.](https://www.railstutorial.org/)** - *Michael Hartl* - *2022* - Learn Rails 7 through three apps; testing, Git, deployment, Hotwire, Turbo; free online.
- **[Agile Web Development with Rails 7.2](https://pragprog.com/titles/rails72/agile-web-development-with-rails-7-2/)** - *Sam Ruby, Dave Thomas, David Heinemeier Hansson* - *2024* - Build a store app with Rails; Hotwire, Stimulus, Turbo, testing, WebSockets.
- **[Programming Ruby 4, 6th Ed.](https://pragprog.com/titles/ruby6/programming-ruby-4-6th-edition/)** - *Noel Rappin, Dave Thomas* - *2024* - The Pickaxe updated for Ruby 4; language, standard library, and ecosystem.
- **[Metaprogramming Ruby 2](https://pragprog.com/titles/ppmetr2/metaprogramming-ruby-2/)** - *Paolo Perrotta* - *2014* - Master Ruby's dynamic nature: blocks, classes, modules, and DSLs.
