# Top Books on Functional Programming

- **[Domain Modeling Made Functional](https://pragprog.com/titles/swdddf/domain-modeling-made-functional/)** - *Scott Wlaschin* - *2018* - Apply FP and DDD together to design business software that's easier to reason about.
- **[Functional Programming in Scala, 2nd Ed.](https://www.manning.com/books/functional-programming-in-scala-second-edition)** - *Paul Chiusano & Rúnar Bjarnason* - *2022* - One of the best “learn FP properly” books: types, purity, and composable design.
- **[Learn You a Haskell for Great Good!](http://learnyouahaskell.com/)** - *Miran Lipovača* - *2011* - The most fun on-ramp to pure FP thinking — free online.
- **[Haskell Programming from First Principles](https://haskellbook.com/)** - *Christopher Allen & Julie Moronuki* - *2016* - Rigorous and beginner-friendly: builds deep understanding of types and functional design.
- **[Category Theory for Programmers](https://bartoszmilewski.com/2014/10/28/category-theory-for-programmers-the-preface/)** - *Bartosz Milewski* - *2019* - The math foundations behind FP concepts, explained for working programmers — free online.
- **[Domain-Driven Design](https://domainlanguage.com/ddd/)** - *Eric Evans* - *2003* - Not an FP book, but pairs extremely well with FP-style modeling of complex domains.
